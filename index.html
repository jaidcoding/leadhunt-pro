One-Click Import from Google Maps
HOW IT WORKS: You drag a button into your Chrome bookmarks bar once. After that, whenever you're on a Google Maps business page, click that bookmark — it opens LeadHunt with the business pre-loaded and ready to import. No typing, no copy-paste.
1
Show your bookmarks bar

Press Ctrl+Shift+B (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+B (Mac) to make it visible.

2
Drag this button into your bookmarks bar
🔖 Send to LeadHunt
← drag this into your bookmarks bar
3
Go to Google Maps and click a business

Open maps.google.com, search for businesses in your city, and click one so its detail panel loads on the left.

4
Click "Send to LeadHunt" in your bookmarks bar

A new LeadHunt tab opens. A popup appears in the bottom-right — click + Add to Pipeline. Done. The site gets auto-scored immediately if they have a website.

Troubleshooting
No popup appears — Check the Pipeline tab, the lead may already be there.
Business name is wrong — Make sure the full business detail panel is loaded on the left in Maps before clicking.
No website detected — Not all businesses list one on Maps. Paste it manually in the Pipeline.
Can't drag the button — Right-click it → "Bookmark this link" → save to Bookmarks Bar.
⚠ Important: This must be hosted (GitHub Pages) for localStorage to persist. Re-drag the bookmarklet from your hosted URL — not this local file.
Where to Find Bad Websites & Emails
Google MapsSearch "[city] + [business type]". Click each result — look for sites that are outdated, broken, or missing entirely.
YelpFilter by category + city. Click through to their website. Lots of broken, non-mobile, or absent sites.
Facebook GroupsSearch "[City] Buy/Sell/Trade" or "[City] Community." Businesses post there with no website — easy pitch.
Hunter.iohunter.io — paste a domain, get the owner's email. Free tier = 25 searches/mo.
PageSpeed Insightspagespeed.web.dev — score under 50 = strong pitch angle. LeadHunt runs this automatically.
Google Search TrickSearch: site:facebook.com "[city]" "[type]" to find businesses that ONLY have a Facebook page.
Chamber of CommerceMost cities publish a free member directory with contact info online. Completely overlooked source.
Nextdoor / ThumbtackLocal service providers already trying to find customers online — very warm leads, often no real website.
SENDING TIPS: Send Mon–Wed mornings. Max 20–30 emails/week to avoid spam flags. Follow up once after 5–7 days. Expect 2–5% reply rate — that's normal and not a reflection on you. Volume + consistency is the game.
